Rent vs. Buy Calculator

How this tool works — how to read the results, how the math works, and where the numbers come from

What you’re looking at

A decision-support tool for the rent-vs-buy question, grounded in live public data. Most rules of thumb are misleading on their own — this tool shows three different views of the same decision side by side, so you can see when they agree (the answer is robust) and when they disagree (you’ve found a real nuance worth thinking about).

Reading each card

  • Home value & Rent — start at the metro median (Zillow ZHVI / ZORI). Click into either field to enter your actual numbers for a specific home you’re considering.
  • Price-to-rent ratio — a quick scan. Above 20 historically favors renting; below 15 favors buying. Useful but blind to mortgage rates and carrying costs.
  • Total monthly cost (buy / rent) — what you actually write checks for each month: mortgage P&I + property tax + maintenance + insurance vs. just rent.
  • NPV breakeven — the smallest number of years you’d need to stay before buying becomes cheaper than renting on a net-present-value basis.
  • At year N (the headline at the bottom) — the bottom-line answer for your specific planned stay duration.

Using the controls

  • How long you plan to stay — sets the year used for the bottom-line answer in each card and a vertical guide on the cumulative-cost chart.
  • Home value growth windows (5y / 10y / 20y / 30y) — pick a historical window for long-run defaults. 20y is the recommended default.
  • Mortgage rate, down payment, investment return and the Advanced settings — manual overrides. Set them to match your own situation.
  • Reset to defaults — restores everything to the data-driven defaults.

How the breakeven math works

The model simulates each year from 1 to 30, tracking what each scenario costs cumulatively if you exited at the end of that year. The breakeven year is the smallest year where buying becomes cheaper than renting on a net basis. Before that year, renting is cheaper. After it, buying is cheaper, and the gap typically widens dramatically.

Where the numbers come from

  • Home values: Zillow ZHVI metro index, refreshed monthly
  • Rents: Zillow ZORI metro index, refreshed monthly
  • Mortgage rate: FRED MORTGAGE30US (Freddie Mac PMMS), weekly
  • Appreciation defaults: FHFA House Price Index by metro, quarterly
  • Rent inflation defaults: BLS CPI Rent of Primary Residence, monthly
  • Property tax rates: US Census ACS effective rates by county
  • Insurance: state-level averages
